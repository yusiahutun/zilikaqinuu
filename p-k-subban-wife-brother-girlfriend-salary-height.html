P. K. Subban has fought his way up to superstar status while doing what he does best. The talented international Canadian hockey player is currently associated with the Nashville Predators of the National Hockey League. The star, who went pro in 2007, was selected by the Montreal Canadiens as the 43rd overall choice in the 2007 NHL draft. Interestingly enough, Subban was named the best defender in the league in 2013. The player also attracted a lot of attention when he switched to the Nashville Predators in 2016 for Shea Weber. The swap is still considered one of the most popular deals in the NHL today. Learn more about him below

P K Subban Biography

Born as Pernell-Karl Sylvester Subban in Toronto, Ontario, Canada; PK was first born on May 13, 1989, He is one of five children of Karl Subban, who worked as a school principal, and his wife Maria. His parents are both Caribbean immigrants to Canada. Growing up, Subban developed an interest in field hockey at a very young age and ended his youth career with the Belleville Bulls of the Ontario Hockey League.

During his junior career, P. K. Subban was selected by the Montreal Canadiens in the 2007 NHL Entry Draft. The talented star had three more seasons with the Bulls before he entered the professional league. When he joined the Canadiens, he signed a three-year rookie contract that expired in 2012. He also shared his season between the Canadiens and their American league subsidiary Hamilton Bulldogs.

Subban received a two-year contract extension worth $5.75 million in January 2013. At the end of the 2013/14 season, he was honored with the James Norris Memorial Trophy as the best defender in the league. He also scored a total of 53 points in 82 appearances during the season. In the same year, he was appointed to the NHL All-Star First. In 2014, Subban signed an eight-year contract with the Canadiens worth $72 million.

In 2016, his team traded Subban for the Nashville Predators for Shea Weber. The star player who joined the Predators led the team to the final of the Stanley Cup playoffs in 2017, scoring 40 points in 66 games. Due to his outstanding leadership skills, he became a finalist for the King Clancy Memorial Trophy. However, the award was later given to Henrik and Daniel Sedin.

PK Subban’s Salary Earnings

The amazing ice hockey star has been very successful since the beginning of his career. Thanks to his wonderful defensive skills, he has earned a huge fortune. Furthermore, the player is currently considered one of the top earners in the league. According to Forbes, PK Subban is the seventh highest-paid player in the field hockey league. He earns up to 11 million dollars in salary and 1.5 million dollars from sponsorship contracts. Subban has signed sponsorship contracts with top brands such as Gatorade, Samsung, and Air Canada, among others.

His other brother Jordan is currently affiliated with the Toronto Marlies of the Canadian Hockey League. Jordan also plays a defensive position for his team.
