Which is the biggest excavator in the world? The top 10 list

June 2024 · 7 minute read

Excavators are intriguing and tremendously handy. The heavy-duty machines are multipurpose and work effectively when mining, digging and moving large objects like soil and debris. You will find these huge machines in any area with activities like road construction, building, demolition, and mining. The biggest excavator in the world is used for weighty jobs that requires intense manpower.

How do you determine the most powerful excavator in the world? The most powerful excavators are determined by features like their operating weight, engines, digging depth, and capacity of tanks. An excavator must be present for any large project to happen as expected.

How much does the biggest excavator in the world cost? Different excavators are designed for different functions, and depending on the strength and specialization needed, the price can be high. The cost of the biggest excavator is priced around $700,000 or more.

Excavators form one of the most in-demand equipment across the construction industry. These machines are extremely versatile and offer a range of choices for size and model. According to Power Truck, here are the biggest machines:

1. Caterpillar 6090 FS

This machine is among the biggest excavators in the world. The Caterpillar 6090 FS excavator weighs 1000 tons, has a length of 13.53 meters, a width of 9.72 meters, and a height of 9.99 meters. Its bucket capacity is 52 cubic meters, while the engine power is 3,360 kW.

This heavy-duty machine has 16 cylinders and a dredging depth of 2.6 meters. Notable features of the caterpillar 6090 FS are its twin-engine, which enhances safety and increases productivity. Its tri-power system is also great as it makes the bulldozer dig more effectively and increases effective lifting force, among other advantages.

2. Bucyrus RH400

This machine is among the largest hydraulic excavators. Currently owned by Caterpillar, the Bucyrus RH400 has a crawler length of 10.98 meters and an undercarriage width of 8.6 meters. The Bucyrus is a front shovel excavator weighing almost 1000 tons.

Why is this excavator captivating? One shovel scoop can take away 45 cubic meters (1589 cubic feet) of rubble. The excavator’s arm crowd force is 3,300 kN with a breakout force of 2,400 kN.

3. Hitachi EX8000-6 expedites excavation

Anyone familiar with Hitachi X5600-6 understands the power of its bigger sibling, The Hitachi EX8000-6. This machine is one of the largest hydraulic excavators today. With a weight of 811 tonnes and the additional shovel and backhoe attachments, no one beats this version of Hitachi.

This machine is 10.5 meters long (34 feet) and with an undercarriage width of 8.65 meters (28 feet). Wondering how big the bucket is? It has a capacity of 45 cubic meters (1589 cubic feet). The Crowding and breakout shovel forces are both 2,870 kN and 2,239 kN.

The Hitachi EX8000-6 excavator is powered by two 16-cylinder Cummins QSKTA60-CE diesel engines, which produce 3,800 horsepower at 1,800 rpm. You will love this excavator more because it is also available in an electric variant.

4. Liebherr R9800

The Liebherr R9800 is a beast. It is made for large-scale mining. With a length of 23.9 meters, a width of 10.5 meters, and a height of 11.7 meters, the German machine is what you need if you are looking for the biggest mining excavator to carry out any maintenance work at your disposal.

Liebherr R9800’s operating weight is 800 tons. The excavator is powered by two 4,000-horsepower engines with 16-V cylinders that enable the excavator to reach a top speed of 2.6 kilometres per hour. The excavator can hold 17500 litres, while its bucket has a capacity of 45 cubic meters. The Liebherr R9800 can dig up to 9 meters and lift 150 tons.

5. The Liebherr R996 B

The Liebherr R996 B is a monster. The sixth largest excavator is a dream for every mining company. The hydraulic excavator weighs 676 toes and 672 tons with the backhoe attachment. Given its size, the excavator’s shovel and backhoe offer a capacity of 26 cubic meters (918 cubic feet).

6. XE7000 XCMG 627

XE7000 ‘XCMG’ is the highest tonnage hydraulic excavator ever made in China. This machine weighs 627 tons and has two engines of 3,600 horsepower. The XE7000 ‘XCMG’ reaches a speed of 1.5 kilometres per hour.

Unlike many machines on this list, the XE7000 ‘XCMG’ does not dig too deep, as its maximum depth is just 3.8 meters. The fuel tank can hold 13 600 litres, while the bucket has a capacity of 36 cubic meters. XE7000 ‘XCMG’ measurements are 23 meters in length, 9 meters in width, and a height of 9.4 meters.

7. Caterpillar 6060

The United States Caterpillar is all you need for large-scale work. This particular Caterpillar 6060 is especially significant as it has an operating weight of 570 tons. The monster has a 3000 horsepower engine that lets it reach a speed of 1.6 kilometres per hour. This bulldozer has a relatively big tank that can hold 13000 litres.

Caterpillar 6060’s bucket has a capacity of 34 cubic meters while its depth is 8.9 meters. The American bulldozer measures 12.5 meters long, 8.7 meters wide, and 8.8 meters high. The machine can lift 67 tons.

8. Komatsu PC 8000-6 240

The Komatsu PC 8000-6 is a Japanese model and one of the most useful machines when it comes to excavation. This machine was primarily designed to load mining trucks of more than 240 tons. It is 14 meters long, 10 meters wide, and 9.6 meters high.
