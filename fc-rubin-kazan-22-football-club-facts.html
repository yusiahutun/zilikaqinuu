Founding and Early Years

FC Rubin Kazan was founded in 1958 and is one of the oldest football clubs in Russia. It started as a team for the workers of a tire manufacturing plant but quickly gained popularity beyond the industrial sector. The club made its way through the lower leagues before achieving promotion to the Russian Premier League in 2003.

Stadium and Capacity

FC Rubin Kazan plays its home games at the Kazan Arena, which has a seating capacity of over 45,000 spectators. The stadium was built for the 2013 Summer Universiade and also hosted matches during the 2018 FIFA World Cup.

Success in Domestic Competitions

FC Rubin Kazan has had several successful seasons in domestic competitions, winning the Russian Premier League twice and the Russian Cup three times. The club’s most memorable achievement came in the 2008 season when they clinched the league title for the first time in their history.
