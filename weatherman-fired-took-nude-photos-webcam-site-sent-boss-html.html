An Emmy-nominated weatherman for Spectrum News NY1 has been fired after a user on an adult webcam site took screenshots of him performing explicitly then sent them to his boss and mother.

Meteorologist Erick Adame took to Instagram on Monday to explain why he had suddenly vanished from the airwaves, saying he had engaged in 'compulsive behavior' inappropriate for a public figure.

'Despite being a public figure and being on television in the biggest market in the country in front of millions of people five days a week for more than a decade and a half, I secretly appeared on an adult webcam website,' Adame wrote in a lengthy confessional post.

'On this site, I acted out my compulsive behaviors, while at home, by performing on camera for other men,' he wrote.

'It was 100% consensual on both of our parts. I wasn't paid for this, and it was absurd of me to think I could keep this private. Nonetheless, my employer found out and I was suspended and then terminated.'

Adame was nominated for two Emmy's throughout his nearly 15-year career, about five of which he spent as a meteorologist at NY1 before his firing.

In his petition filed in a Manhattan court on Monday, Adame asked the judge that the webcam site - only identified as Unit 4 Media, Ltd. - be forced to reveal identifying information about the individual who leaked the images so he can pursue a lawsuit against them.

The petition says in late 2021 Adame used the 'video chat service' on the Unit 4 website and that an anonymous user 'wrongfully copied unclothed images' of him 'by taking screenshots.'

The filing says the user then 'wrongfully disseminated the images' to his mother and employer 'with the intent of harassing, annoying, or alarming'  and 'tortiously interfering' with his employment.
