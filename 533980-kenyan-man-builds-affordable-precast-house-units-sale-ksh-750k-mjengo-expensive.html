Kenyan Man Builds Affordable Precast House Units for Sale at KSh 750k: &quot;Mjengo Is Expensive&quot;

August 2024 · 6 minute read
  • The government announced plans to build 250,000 affordable house units every year to curb the two million deficit in the country
  • President William Ruto's administration said the programme will give Kenyans access to decent and affordable houses
  • A two-bedroom house under the government affordable housing programme costs KSh 3.15 million
  • However, according to Amos Kibaru, a real estate developer, this amount can be cheaper for Kenyans if they adapt manufactured precast units

TUKO.co.ke journalist Wycliffe Musalia brings over five years of experience in financial, business, and technology reporting, offering deep insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

What is an affordable house? Many Kenyans are not quite sure about the answer to this question.

But for Amos Kibaru, a real estate developer, an affordable house is one that demands less construction cost from the owner—from the building foundation to the complete and finished unit.

How much is Ruto's affordable house unit?

President William Ruto's administration has been running the affordable housing programme, which promises Kenyans decent homes for an average of between KSh 3.15 million (2-bedroom) and KSh 4.25 million (3-bedroom).

Ruto promised to build 250,000 housing units yearly to offset Kenya's two-million housing deficit.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, Kibaru explained that his precast house manufacturing plant - located in Kikuyu, Kiambu County - can produce 200 units per month.

I can do 200 units per month. A typical mwananchi who is not a civil servant and has bought land somewhere now needs to build. Will the government build on his land? No. That is where we as private entrepreneurs are coming it to bridge the gap in the affordable housing agenda," said Kibaru

He demystified Ruto's affordable housing programme, saying it only favours most salaried people, including civil servants.

"...if you are not one of them, then you're off the board," he noted.

What is precast house, and how much is construction cost?

According to Construction Kenya, a precast or prefabricated house is a building manufactured using technology, with its skeletons made in a factory off-site before it is transported to the site for assembly.

Kibaru revealed that the precast materials are manufactured and assembled within 21 days at a cost of KSh 750,000 for a 3-bedroom house with a big lounge and kitchen.

"We have hit the market with affordable houses that will cost below KSh 2 million in a short period - for 21 days. "We did a prototype in Kiambu, a 3-bedroom 75 square metres house. It cost KSh 750,000 to build... the house shell done in 11 days," he said.

The Floor Decor Kenya Ltd owner estimated the house finishing cost to be around KSh 1.3 million and KSh 1.95 million, based on your preference and budget.

Features of precast house unit

He said the house units are categorised based on the client's preference and budget.

For a Promax house unit, the internal finishes include a Mkeka-Mbao floor and a gypsum ceiling.

The house also has a bio-digester, a 20,000-litre precast underground membrane tank to store water and a Solar water heater.

The construction entrepreneur affirmed high quality since the precast units are produced in a controlled environment.

How precast houses are made

Kibaru noted that he ventured into the manufacturing of precast house units two years ago as demand for affordable houses grew.

The developer noted that precast houses are significant in cutting down the cost of construction.

"Two years ago, we ventured into production... manufacturing is the way to go. We started producing houses at a factory... precast concrete houses.

"We started with slabs. Slab construction is tedious work that involves many things like cutting trees to support it, there is a lot of labour involved and takes days to complete, with a lot of undetermined costs," he explained.

He revealed that his Kikuyu-based factory produces T beams for flooring and precasts the foundation.

This eliminates all the costs related to building the foundation from excavation.

The precast columns are mounted to the soil-bearing capacity level ( where the soil is dry).

"You only excavate where the columns will be, and then after elevating them, suspend the foundation," he continued.

The foundation is done within two days, and then precast interlocking blocks or panels are used for the walls.

Are precast house units affordable?

Kibaru noted that precast wall panels do not need plaster but painting for finishing.

They are hollow to avoid sound travelling from one room to the other, acting as insulation for coolness.

"The most expensive part of mjengo (construction) today is labour. Workers take their time so that they can earn more from a project, which precast houses try to eliminate. "To bring the cost down, we deviated from pitched roofs many Kenyans have been doing to a flat or butterfly roof. This reduces the cost of roofing by over 70%." he added.

Precast house manufacturing reduces tree cutting to support slab construction, making it environmentally friendly.

Challenges related to precast house construction

Kibaru cited the volatility of the forex market, saying that the continued fall of the Kenya shilling against the dollar affects the prices of materials.

"We have had increases in cement prices more than 10 times... making the prices of precast materials high."Transportation cost is high when transporting the house to far regions... until the concept is adapted throughout the country. We need to build more factories for precast houses in Kenya."

Is Ruto's taxation plan affecting construction sector?

He also cited issues with increased government levies and taxation to the construction industry, which he termed as Kenya's biggest employer.

"We are demystifying the construction sector with precast, giving builders a predetermined cost. We are working closely with the county governments to obtain approvals for bungalows, they are not storey buildings that will require approval every time."I want every Kenyan to own a home in an affordable manner," he concluded.
