Funny Farm Home for Sale

April 2024 · 2 minute read

"Remember, Mrs. Farmer. Whenever you buy a house, whatever's in the ground belongs to you—whether it's gold or oil... or Claude Musselman." — Sheriff Ledbetter

If you fell in love with the idea of small town life while watchingFunny Farm (even as you observed Elizabeth Farmer dig up the body of her home's former owner in the garden), you are not alone. Despite the trials and tribulations faced by the Farmers after moving to their country Cape in the fictional town of Redbud, Vermont, something about the charm of New England hooked them in the end. And who can blame them? The film's setting is nothing if not idyllic. And one of its most memorable scenes was filmed right in Townshend, Vermont, in the barn behind the Old Brick Tavern—a circa 1792, federal-style B&B that just happens to be for sale. Are you ready to live the dream?

The barn was the site of quirky Ethel Dinges' antique store, where Elizabeth shopped for furnishings for the house. Except for the smaller sign, it looks pretty much the same!

The antique store in a scene from Funny Farm

The current owners must know a thing or two about antiques, too, as they've decked out the home's interior with an array of elegant period furnishings. It just might be the perfect holiday retreat.

As for the Farmers' Cape, that home still stands in the nearby town of Grafton.

82 Common Road in Townshend, Vermont is on the market for an asking price of $329,000. For more information, contact listing agent John McPherson at (802) 254-6400 or visit Berkley & Veller Greenwood Country Realtors.

Writer Elizabeth Finkelstein is a self-proclaimed old house addict on a lifelong hunt for her perfect historic home. From big Victorian fixer-uppers to tiny colonials to mid-century modern masterpieces, Elizabeth believes that the best homes show the charm of having been loved over time. She chronicles it all on her website CIRCA Old Houses, which showcases beautiful old houses for sale across the country. See more at CircaOldHouses.com.
