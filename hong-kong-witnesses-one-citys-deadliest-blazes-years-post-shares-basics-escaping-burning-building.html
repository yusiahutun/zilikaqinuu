What are the basics?

Preparation is essential. Familiarise yourself with your building’s exits and escape routes. Make sure stairways and passages are not blocked off or sealed.

In the event of a fire, it is important to stay calm to ensure you can react appropriately.

If a blaze breaks out in your flat or immediate space, alert everyone there and tell them to get out quickly. If there is a break-glass unit in the corridor, break it and activate the fire alarm.

Escape using the nearest and safest hallways and stairways. Avoid using routes heavily affected by smoke. Do not use any lifts.

When you are safe, dial “999” to report the fire.

If possible, the Fire Services Department recommends bringing three key items – your mobile phone, keys and a wet towel.

What if the fire is coming from downstairs?

While evacuating a burning building should always be the first course of action and the ultimate goal, it may not be possible in certain situations.

If fire or smoke is coming from below, you may need to head to your building’s rooftops for temporary refuge.

Lam Kin-kwan, commander of the Fire Services Department’s Kowloon South division, said people should first check the severity of any smoke in a stairwell, and switch paths if it is too heavy.

“Because the smoke often contains toxic substances when inhaled, such as carbon monoxide, which can easily cause suffocation, burns or poisoning, or lead to unconsciousness,” he said.

Staying put?

If the smoke has become too thick, try to find your way back to your home or another safe place.

Close the door and seal the gaps with duct tape and wet towels or tissues to keep out the smoke. Tell emergency services of your exact location to ensure your speedy rescue.

Stay by the balcony or a space with the least amount of smoke. When you are safe, try to catch the attention of rescuers by signalling with a towel or a bedsheet.

Which materials are fire hazards?

The Fire Services Department does not recommend storing highly flammable objects at home, such as petrol, kerosene and thinners.

During renovations, materials such as paints, thinners and other flammable liquids should also be handled with care.

If you have recently used a polyurethane polish in your house or workspace, open all your windows to help dissipate the flammable gas before turning on the power or lighting a fire.

In the aftermath of the fire at New Lucky House, authorities said they suspected an abundance of plastic materials left over from construction work and large amounts of other waste, such as plastic bags, could have contributed to the heavy smoke.

How to prepare for or prevent a fire?

In addition to knowing your escape routes and keeping them clear, the Fire Services Department encourages building tenants to attend safety seminars and drills.

Occupants should also install individual smoke detectors and keep a portable extinguisher for small fires.

Ensure smoke doors are unlocked and unobstructed but shut.

Fire service installations should be inspected at least once a year, and repaired immediately if they are damaged or not working properly.
