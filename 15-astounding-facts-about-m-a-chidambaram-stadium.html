The oldest cricket stadium in India

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium, commonly known as Chepauk Stadium, is the oldest cricket stadium in India. Its rich history dates back to its establishment in 1916, making it an iconic landmark in the world of cricket.

Capacity of over 50,000 spectators

With a seating capacity of over 50,000 spectators, M. A. Chidambaram Stadium is one of the largest cricket stadiums in India. It has witnessed numerous memorable matches and has hosted international cricket matches for decades.

Home ground of the Tamil Nadu cricket team

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium serves as the home ground for the Tamil Nadu cricket team. It has been the venue for several domestic matches, including Ranji Trophy, Duleep Trophy, and Indian Premier League (IPL) matches held by the Chennai Super Kings (CSK).

Named after M. A. Chidambaram

The stadium is named after M. A. Chidambaram, a former President of the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) and a prominent figure in the development of cricket in Chennai. His contributions to the sport are commemorated through this iconic stadium.

Known for hosting exciting IPL matches

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium is famous for hosting adrenaline-pumping IPL matches. The electrifying atmosphere and the passionate crowd make it a thrilling experience for both players and spectators.

Witnessed memorable matches and performances

The stadium has witnessed several historic moments in cricket history. It has seen exceptional performances by legendary players like Sachin Tendulkar, Rahul Dravid, and Virender Sehwag, leaving an indelible mark on the cricketing world.

The iconic “V-Guard” stand

The stadium features a standout structure called the “V-Guard” stand, known for its distinct shape and design. This stand adds to the architectural beauty of the stadium and provides an excellent view of the cricket action.

Hosted the first-ever Test match in India

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium holds the honor of hosting the first-ever Test match played in India. The match took place in 1934 between India and England, marking a significant milestone in the nation’s cricket history.

A batting paradise

The pitch at M. A. Chidambaram Stadium is known to favor batsmen. The clay-based pitch offers good bounce and provides an ideal setting for strokeplay, often resulting in high-scoring matches and thrilling cricketing contests.

A hub for cricketing talent

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium has been a breeding ground for talented cricketers. Many renowned players have honed their skills at this venue and gone on to represent the national team, leaving a lasting impact on the sport.

Renovated with world-class facilities

The stadium underwent significant renovations to upgrade its facilities to international standards. The revamp included modern dressing rooms, media boxes, state-of-the-art floodlights, and improved seating arrangements for the comfort of spectators.

A historic venue for cricket rivalries

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium has witnessed intense cricket rivalries over the years. Matches between India and Pakistan, India and Australia, and other cricketing nations have graced this hallowed ground, creating unforgettable moments in the game.

The electric atmosphere of the “Chepauk Roar”

The passionate fans at M. A. Chidambaram Stadium generate an electrifying atmosphere, often referred to as the “Chepauk Roar.” The crowd’s enthusiasm and unwavering support create a captivating ambiance that adds to the overall cricketing experience.

Honored with the “Venue of the Year” award

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium has been recognized as the “Venue of the Year” by the International Cricket Council (ICC) multiple times. This award reflects the stadium’s exceptional facilities, management, and ability to host world-class cricket events.

A beloved symbol of Chennai’s cricketing legacy

M. A. Chidambaram Stadium holds a special place in the hearts of Chennai’s cricket enthusiasts. It represents the city’s vibrant cricket culture, passion for the sport, and the enduring legacy of cricket in this part of the country.
