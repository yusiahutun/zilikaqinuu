One Georgia mother remained remarkably calm when she walked into the kitchen and found that her three-year-old daughter had completely covered her 18-month-old son in peanut butter.

The proud big sister, Emily, had somehow gotten her hands on an enormous container of peanut butter, and proceeded to slather it all over her little brother, Ethan - painting him with the sticky substance from his head right down to his toes.

Their mom, Gina Gardner Brown, 44, recently shared a video she took of the mischievous moment on Facebook, explaining that it occurred over a decade ago - but is no less hysterical to watch.

So sticky! Gina Gardner Brown shared a video on Facebook showing two of her children making a mess with peanut butter

Clean-up time: The Georgia mother caught her daughter Emily, then three, smothering her son Ethan, then 18 months, in the gooey food

'What are you two doing?' asks Gina's horrified voice from behind the camera, as she films her children reveling in the mess on top of the kitchen table, before asking again: 'What are you doing? 

Emily's smile falters as she hears her mom's unimpressed tone, and she rubs her peanut-butter-covered hands together.

'I don't know,' she answers, the smile cautiously creeping back onto her peanut-butter-smudged face, as she quickly surveys Ehtan sitting next to her. 'I was just scrubbing him.'

Ethan, meanwhile, simply stares at his mom, silently, one eye plastered shut by globs of peanut butter.

So that's fun? Emily admits that she isn't sure why she covered her brother in peanut butter but seems to be enjoying herself

Goo-goo ga-ga, indeed: Ethan seems to be having a good time too, communicating his content to his mom in babbling syllables

Parenthood: Gina also shows off the mess that her children made in the rest of the kitchen

'Oh, Ethan!' Gina says, unable to hold back her laughter - to which Ethan responds with some cute, unintelligible baby noises, perhaps trying to explain how he managed to get roped into his sister's scheme.

'I knew you two must be getting into something, but I just didn't imagine that it would be this,' she adds.

Emily - realizing that she doesn't seem to be getting in trouble after all - rubs more peanut butter between her fingers before wiping it down the side of her brother's face.

Taking it in stride: Gina mostly laughs at the scene, though she calmly tells her daughter that she can't do this again

Play time: Adorably, Ethan begins smacking his stomach with his own hand full of peanut butter

'Does it feel good?' Gina asks, giggling. Ethan responds with a single baby-talk syllable, but seems to be answering in the affirmative.

But when Gina tells him he has to go in the bath, the toddler seems less enthusiastic.

'Emily, no more,' she then tells her daughter, before again announcing that it's bath time.

'I'm gonna take a bath on my hands!' Emily says excitedly, as Gina surveys the peanut butter damage to the rest of the kitchen, showing that Emily also managed to smear the sticky substance all over the table.

Got some scrubbing to do: The two cute kids are told at the end of the video that it's time for a bath

All grown up: Gina recorded the video in 2004; Emily, now in her teens (top left), and Ethan, now in his preteens or early teens, are presumably no longer dipping their hands into the peanut butter

Gina then swings around to the other side of the table to capture the rest of her daughter's handiwork, patiently telling her: 'Emily, you're an artist - but let's not do this again, OK? This is not something we should repeat.'

When Emily points out that she put peanut brother on Ethan's tummy - ignoring the fact that the entire rest of his face and body are covered as well - Ethan dips his own hand into the peanut butter jar and stars lightly smacking his belly with the mess. The two children then clap together, laughing.

Now in her early teens, Emily no longer seems to be getting into the peanut butter jar. Ethan, who is likely to be 12 or 13, is similarly mess-free in his mother's recent photos.
