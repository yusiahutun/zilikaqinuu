Who was Danny Kaye?

He was a talented personality in the American entertainment industry, wearing the hats of an actor, singer, comedian, and dancer. Danny was born on 18th January 1911 in New York City, United States, to Jacob and Clara Kaminsky.

His parents were Ukrainian-Jewish immigrants. He had two elder brothers, Larry and Mac Kaminsky. He attended Public School 149 in East New York, Brooklyn. Later, he enrolled at the Thomas Jefferson High School in Brooklyn but never graduated.

What was Danny Kaye famous for?

Danny was famous for his successful film and music career. Also, he was the first ambassador-at-large of UNICEF in 1954. In 1986, he received the French Legion of Honour for his years of work with the organization.

How did Danny Kaye die?

He succumbed to a heart attack on 3rd March 1987, at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. His death was caused by internal bleeding and complications from hepatitis C. In February 1983, he had undergone heart surgery, during which he contracted hepatitis C from a blood transfusion.
