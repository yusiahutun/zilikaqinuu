Spice Girls are 'updating some of their songs' before 2019 reunion 'so they don't offend anyone'

As 2019 draws closer, so does the Spice Girls' latest reunion tour, which is set for May/June next year. 

But as the girls - Mel C, Geri Horner, Mel C and Emma Bunton [not Victoria Beckham] - gear up to hit rehearsals next month, reports are suggesting that they're considering changing some of their riskier lyrics.

Notably, the line 'Yellow man in Timbuktu/Colour for both me and you' from their sixth single Spice Up Your Life is potentially on the chopping block, with worries that it is now considered racist.

Released originally in 1997, the 20-year gap is said to be worrying the girls, according to The Sun.

'The reality is it’s not acceptable to say that now,' a source told the publication. 'Some of the girls feel uncomfortable getting up on stage and performing that lyric, especially when they have such a diverse and multicultural fanbase.

The line on the album version sang 'Any deal that we endeavour/Boys and girls feel good together' and was voiced by Geri.

For the single version - which went on to be the first of the band's Christmas number one tracks - Victoria took the vocals, and they were changed to 'Once again if we endevour/Love will bring us back together'.

It was said that Geri didn't feel happy singing in the key required, but the change in wording suggested they lyrics were re-thought because it would alienate gay people.

There's plenty of questions in the air surrounding the girls' forthcoming tour and the lyrics - namely because Victoria won't be on hand to sing her lines. Equally, the tracks the band released after Geri left the group temporarily will need to be sung by her, rather than Victoria.

Tacks like Holler, Goodbye and Let Love Lead The Way all feature solos by Victoria, as do a plethora of tracks from their third album, Forever, which was made without Geri.

Geri rejoined the band in 2007 for their first reunion. The Return Of The Spice Girls tour was worldwide and mostly featured songs that were released as singles.
