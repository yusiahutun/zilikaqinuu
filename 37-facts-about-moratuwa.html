Moratuwa is a city located in the Western Province of Sri Lanka.

Surrounded by the Indian Ocean to the west and the Bolgoda Lake to the east, Moratuwa offers breathtaking views of nature.

The name “Moratuwa” translates to “peacock land” in Sinhalese.

This beautiful coastal city is known for its vibrant culture and artistic heritage.

Moratuwa is renowned for its skilled craftsmen and artisans.

The city is famous for its woodwork, particularly in creating intricate furniture and wood carving designs.

The University of Moratuwa is one of the leading technical universities in Sri Lanka.

Established in 1972, it offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in engineering, architecture, and other fields.

Moratuwa is home to the iconic Katubedda Campus of the University of Moratuwa.

This campus specializes in technology and is known for its advanced research facilities.

The city is renowned for its vibrant music and dance traditions.

Moratuwa is famous for its Baila music, a unique genre blending Portuguese and Sri Lankan influences.

Moratuwa is known for its picturesque beaches.

Mount Lavinia Beach, located in close proximity, is a popular spot for locals and tourists to relax and enjoy the sunsets.

The city is home to numerous churches and temples, reflecting its multicultural heritage.

St. Sebastian’s Church and Kadalana Raja Maha Viharaya are among the notable religious landmarks in Moratuwa.

Moratuwa is a major hub for the fishing industry in Sri Lanka.

The coastal location provides ample opportunities for fishermen to catch a wide variety of fish and seafood.

The Moratuwa Municipal Council governs the city.

Moratuwa is famous for its vibrant street food culture.

The city is a hub for traditional mask-making.

Moratuwa is known for its intricate and colorful traditional masks, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Sri Lanka.

Moratuwa is home to the well-known Prince of Wales’ College.

Established in 1876, it is one of the oldest and prestigious educational institutions in Sri Lanka.

The Bolgoda Lake in Moratuwa is the largest freshwater lake in Sri Lanka.

It serves as a popular water sports destination, attracting boating and jet skiing enthusiasts.

Moratuwa is famous for its vibrant arts and crafts industry.

Locals produce beautiful handmade items such as pottery, batik textiles, and traditional jewelry.

The city hosts the annual Moratuwa Arts Forum, showcasing the talents of local artists and performers.

FAQs

1. What are some popular attractions in Moratuwa?

Some popular attractions in Moratuwa include De Soysa Stadium, Moratumulla Lighthouse, and St. Sebastian’s Church.

2. Can you engage in water sports activities in Moratuwa?

Yes, Moratuwa offers opportunities for water sports activities like swimming, surfing, and snorkeling at its beautiful beaches.

3. Is Moratuwa known for its handicrafts?

Absolutely! Moratuwa is famous for its intricate woodwork craftsmanship, which you can explore at places like the Laksala Handicrafts Emporium.

4. Are there any annual festivals celebrated in Moratuwa?

Yes, the Moratumulla Perahera is a popular religious festival celebrated annually in Moratuwa, featuring colorful processions and cultural performances.

5. Are there any renowned educational institutions in Moratuwa?

Yes, Moratuwa is home to the University of Moratuwa, renowned for its programs in engineering, architecture, and technology.

6. How is the local cuisine in Moratuwa?

The local cuisine in Moratuwa is diverse and flavorful, with an emphasis on seafood dishes like fish curry, prawn curry, and crab curry.
