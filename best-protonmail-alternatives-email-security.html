The 8 Best ProtonMail Alternatives for Your Email Security

July 2024 Â· 5 minute read

Although ProtonMail is the largest secure email provider on the market, some users may find its features unappealing, despite its ability to ensure extreme data security.

There are now several email platforms competing with ProtonMail that offers just as good, if not better, security for your email. Here are a few ProtonMail alternatives that you can check out and understand how they protect your online data.

1. Tutanota

Highly touted as one of the best privacy-focused alternatives to ProtonMail, Tutanota provides both iOS and Android users with an encrypted way to manage their email.

It encrypts all of your data, so snoopers can't use it for their commercial purposes. The platform claims that even admins cannot decrypt or read your data.

It's free to use the basic secure email plan. You don't need to provide a phone number or personal information to register for an account.

Additionally, Tutanota offers a fully encrypted calendar and contacts service. For beginners looking to learn more about online privacy, this platform is a great place to start.

2. Disroot

Disroot offers online services based on freedom, privacy, federation, and decentralization. They do this by removing any tracking, ads, profiling, and data mining from your account.

Instead of relying on investments or corporations to support their work, Disroot runs off donations and community support. They have a variety of services, such as emails, spreadsheets, cloud, and forums, for securing your data.

The Android app is available on their site and has all of the services they offer. Depending on the service you need, you may not even have to register.

3. Posteo

Posteo is an independently-run email service in Berlin, Germany, where data protection laws are more strict than in other countries. Their service comes with up to 2GB of storage, but you can upgrade to as much as 20 GB.

Posteo works with all smartphones, tablets, PCs, and registration doesn't require any personal information. Posteo even developed its own payment system.

Posteo takes no money from advertisers, so you will not see any ads when you use their service or visit their site. All of their services are free with customizable options. The only downside is the absence of customer support and custom domain options.

4. Mailfence

Mailfence has been around since 2013. It is an excellent alternative to ProtonMail as it has a completely free and fully encrypted email system.

The service is private, which means you won't be tracked, spammed, or subjected to government surveillance. They're based in Belgium, which has well-enforced privacy laws.

With digitally signed emails, you can ensure that it was sent only by you, and not by someone else. Besides an encrypted email, you also get privacy-focused messages, calendars, documents, and contacts.

Mailfence's platform is browser-based and doesn't need any extensions or plugins. Mailfence could improve its service by offering open-source code, but overall, it's worth considering.

5. Fastmail

Fastmail is the only email encryption service on this list that doesn't include a free plan. It is accessible from a computer, smartphone, and PC.

You can also encrypt your email, calendar, and contacts using this platform. If you're looking to keep your primary email address private, Fastmail offers multiple custom domain email addresses from one account.

You can give off the impression of professionalism while still remaining private. Fastmail does not track your personal data, so it has no ads. It offers a free 30-day trial for you to use their platform, but it does not offer end-to-end encryption.

6. Vivaldi Mail

Vivaldi added a mail client service to their platform in November of 2020 in response to Opera dropping their mail web client.

Unlike ProtonMail, the new platform offers direct PGB-based email encryption as well as the ability to import your own PGB keys.

Vivaldi mail is not quite as advanced as the other email platforms on this list and still requires a previous email and phone number to begin. It is available on all browsers and Android devices.

7. Mailpile

The Mailpile platform is totally free and funded by its community, which allows it to remain true to its mission of protecting your privacy. The software is open-source and displays no advertisements while in use.

You can use Mailpile even if the internet is down and it is faster than cloud services. You can also use its search function to find your emails quickly. It encrypts all your emails with open PGP signatures.

You can download Mailpile as a browser add-on and use it with macOS, Windows, and Linux operating systems.

8. Barracuda

Barracuda is a well-established company that offers customers protected email services. They have also developed a number of other protected services, such as application and cloud security, network security, and data protection.

Because Barracuda is targeted towards businesses, it is not an obvious replacement for ProtonMail as their primary service does not provide encryption. Instead, Barracuda aims to protect your email from 13 of the most dangerous email threats.

Barracuda protects you from digital threats, including spam, phishing, and malicious websites. However, Barracuda is not the most secure email solution out there.

Take Advantage of These ProtonMail Alternatives

These ProtonMail alternatives give you just as much privacy and security as ProtonMail, if not more.

All of these platforms offer encrypted services, and many of them are crowd-funded, which helps make your privacy a priority. Now that your email is secure, it's time to secure your online browsing as well.
