Parenthood & Early Career of Joe Burrow

Joe Burrow was born Joseph Lee Burrow in Iowa, United States. He was born on December 10, 1986. His nationality is American, and he belongs white ethnicity. 

As mentioned above, Joe is the son of Jim Burrow, a former NFL player, and college football coach.

Likewise, Burrow is younger than his two older brothers, who played football in Nebraska. You surely might have heard of Jamie Burrow and Dan Burrow. 

The NFL star comes from a lineage of athletes, his uncle also played football at Ole Miss, and his grandfather was a basketball star.

High School Career of “The Tiger King,” Aka Joe Burrow

He grew up with a passion for sports; he started playing football at six. He started learning football under the supervision of his Father.

Unlike his Father, uncle, and older brothers, who played defense, he began as a quarterback.

Burrow went to Athens High School, Ohio, for his early football career.

He had 11,416 yards with 157 passing touchdowns throughout his career at Athens and rushed for 2,067 yards with 27 rushing touchdowns.

Because of his remarkable performance, Burrow was honored with the Mr. Football Award and Gatorade Player of the Year Award in 2014.

He was not only limited to just one sport. He was also a good basketball player. However, in the end, he chose football as his future goal.

2020 NFL Draft and Journey with Cincinnati Bengals

For Burrow, football is like a family affair; his ancestors all were from football backgrounds. Especially his dad was a great player in this game.

After the Heisman Trophy and National Championship, now the journey he has to begin with the NFL team Cincinnati Bengals.

Burrow was one of the 58 players who went for the NFL draft in 2020. He was selected following Baker Mayfield and Kyler Murray.

On July 31, 2020, he agreed on a four-year $36.1 million contract with the Bengals.

He threw for 193 passing yards and an interception in his debut game and had 46 rushing yards in a 16-3 loss against Los Angeles Chargers.

In his next game, he performed exceptionally well against Cleveland Browns. However, his team lost the match.

Two weeks later, he bagged his first win against the Jacksonville Jaguars with a score of 33-25. He was also the first rookie quarterback who threw for 300 yards in three straight games.

The quarterback was the first rookie in NFL history with 400 passing yards, three passing touchdowns, and a rushing touchdown in one game. 

Burrow Injury 

On November 22, 2020, he tore his anterior cruciate ligament while passing the ball while playing with the Washington Football Team.

Joe also injured his medial collateral ligament in the left side of his knee and other damage to the posterior cruciate ligament and meniscus.

On December 2, 2020, he underwent successful surgery and was expected to make a full recovery.
