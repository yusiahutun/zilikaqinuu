Career

After graduating from the University of Sioux Falls, DeBoer returned to Washington High School in Sioux Falls, South Dakota, as an assistant coach. He spent two years there before reconnecting with Bob Young, landing his old role as offensive coordinator at his alma mater from 2000 to 2004.

When Young retired on December 1, 2004, DeBoer was promoted to head coach. From 2005 to 2009, he led the team, securing an impressive overall score of 6703 and clinching three NAIA national championships in 2006, 2008, and 2009, with a runner-up position in 2007.

He faced losses to Mike Van Diest, whose Carroll Fighting Saints won in 2005 and 2007. Transitioning to coaching positions, he served as offensive coordinator at Southern Illinois (2010-2013) under Dale Lennon and then at Eastern Michigan University (2014-2016) with Chris Creighton.

In 2017 and 2018, he held the offensive coordinator role at Fresno State under Jeff Tedford before moving to Indiana in 2019, where his team ranked second in the Big Ten with an average of 443.6 yards per game.

In 2020, DeBoer became the head football coach at California State University, Fresno, until November 29, 2021, when he joyfully accepted the position of head football coach at the University of Washington.
