Lauren James breaks silence after red card stamp and says 'sorry' for moment of madness

April 2024 · 3 minute read

Lauren James has apologised for her reckless stamp on Nigeria star Michelle Alozie.

James was sent off late on during England's Women's World Cup last-16 clash against Nigeria on Monday. The Chelsea star appeared to lose her cool and let emotions get the better of her on a frustrating evening for the Lionesses, who eventually made it through to the quarter-finals on penalties.

James was sent off after a VAR review adjudged her to have stamped on Alozie's back in the 87th minute. The 21-year-old forward has now spoken out about the incident, quote-tweeting a tweet from Alozie herself.

Alozie wrote: "Abeg, rest. We are playing on the world’s stage. This game is one of passion, insurmountable emotions, and moments. All respect for Lauren James.."

James replied by apologising to both Alozie and her England team-mates and fans. She wrote: "All my love and respect to you. I am sorry for what happened. Also, for our England fans and my team-mates, playing with and for you is my greatest honour and I promise to learn from my experience."

Lionesses fans rushed to the comments section to praise the humility of the England star. One wrote: "Class on class. Alozie and James get it."

Someone else said: "Now that how to make an apology. Good person." While a third commented: "Huge respect for both players here! I think everyone needs to let go, she is human."

An FA spokesperson released a statement on Tuesday too, confirming they hope to ensure James isn't handed a three-game ban. It read: "Lauren is really sorry for her actions which led to the red card and is full of remorse. It is wholly out of character for her.

"We will be supporting Lauren throughout and will be putting forward representation on her behalf. We fully respect FIFA's disciplinary process and will not be making any further comment until after any decision has been made."

England boss Sarina Wiegman said after the game that the incident would be a learning curve for James. She said: "It's a huge lesson for her to learn. She would never want to hurt someone. She is the sweetest person I know. It was late in the game so players get a little tired.

"She is inexperienced on this stage and in a split-second lost her emotions. It isn't something she did on purpose. She apologised and felt really bad."

James had lit up the tournament until her red card, scoring twice and providing three assists in England's 6-1 win over China in the group stages. However, she will now be missing for the quarter-final against Colombia on Saturday.

And there could be more bad news with FIFA able to extend her ban if they see fit. An extra two-game ban would mean the end of her tournament, even if the Lionesses reach the final Down Under.
