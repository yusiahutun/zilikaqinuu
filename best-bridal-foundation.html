Best bridal foundation for your wedding day recommended by make-up artists

May 2024 · 7 minute read

On your wedding day it might seem like an infinite amount of variables loom outside of your control. While, unfortunately, we haven't figured out a way to control the weather or pre-empt which family member will make an unfiltered comment in their speech, there are certain things you can ensure go smoothly – not least your foundation.

Just ask Taylor Hill, the latest celebrity bride to reveal the products she wore on her Big Day. Among them was one of world's most-loved foundations, Armani's Luminous Silk. Her make-up artist Carolina Gonzalez mixed two shades together (5.75 and 5.8) and applied it with a fluffy foundation brush for a diffused finish.

To provide the most comprehensive overview of how to choose the perfect wedding day foundation for you, we asked expert bridal make-up artists Hannah Martin, Kelly Dawn, Amy Conway and Cher Webb to impart their expertise and experience, as well as share the foundations they keep in their kits.

Tip 1: Test-running your foundation will save tears on the day

In an age of social media where we've become unavoidably aware of how our make-up looks in photos, which, aside from the element of over-self-analysing, can serve to give us a better idea of which foundations work well with the camera and which don't.

"Too much luminosity in a foundation and you can look a little shiny, almost metallic, whereas totally matte can make your skin look flat," says Hannah Martin. "I like to use a more dewy foundation but set all the key ‘hotspots’ well with powder – these being areas that are prone to shine like the T-zone, above the brows, around the nose and chin."

Which leads us to the key takeaway: always test-run your foundations before the wedding. Even if the foundation looks great in the shop, it's crucial to wear it for at least a few days to see how different environments can impact the finish. Foundations will sit differently depending on your skin type, and by testing beforehand, you can see whether or not the foundation oxidises, how long it lasts on the skin, and if it requires touch-ups throughout the day.

If you're happy with the coverage and longevity, but not the luminosity level, "You can always add an illuminating moisturiser underneath or extra powder on top to manipulate the finish," suggests Amy Conway.

Tip 2: More coverage isn't necessarily always better

A common misconception when it comes to bridal foundation is that you have to choose between what looks best on the day and what looks best in photos, often resulting in people applying more foundation than is necessary to appear 'smoother' on camera.

"For weddings, I don’t believe a bride should pile on more make-up than they are used to, just stand out in photos," says Kelly Dawn. "Make-up can be fresh, natural and still stand out beautifully if you use the right products and techniques. Wedding photography usually doesn't have the same harsh lighting as studio shoots so make-up doesn’t need to be amped up so much."

"Coverage is mainly down to personal preference, however I do think enough coverage to banish any unwanted redness is key. Red is a very jealous colour, so if there is redness in the skin it can steal focus from other areas you’re hoping to enhance," advises Martin.

Spotlighting with coverage is a good alternative to going all-over with a heavier foundation that you're not necessarily comfortable wearing. Martin uses MAC's Face and Body Foundation as an all-over base, then takes Bobbi Brown's Skin Foundation Stick to add coverage in the particular areas that require it – usually the nose, chin and front of the cheeks.

Tip 3: How you apply your foundation makes the world of difference

"It’s so important to apply your foundation with a brush on your wedding day," says Cher Webb. "No matter how clean our hands are, we still have natural oils on our finger tips and the excess oils can cause foundation to slip throughout the day. A foundation brush will ensure you get an even, buildable application that looks flawless and lasts."

To ensure you're left with a natural and even finish, go over your base with a sponge as a final touch (before powder) to remove any excess foundation and help it adhere better to your skin.

Tip 4: Ensure bridesmaids are armed with a mini touch-up kit

Emotions run high on your wedding day, so tears are often inevitable. To protect your base, make sure your bridesmaids have the right tools on hand, should you need them.

"The one thing I recommend all of my brides have handy is a good setting powder to touch up throughout the day and avoid hotspots in your photos," says Dawn. "Photography lighting can enhance the shine and if you haven’t powdered in the key areas (forehead, around the nose, T-zone) you might end up looking more sweaty than glowy. I love the Charlotte Tilbury Airbrush Flawless Finish Powder for this."

Tip 5: Prepping your base will make all the difference

"Skincare makes a huge difference to how your make-up looks and lasts," says Conway. "Opt for a primer that adds radiance to ensure you achieve a natural glow that will last all day for you. I love to use vitamin C in bridal skincare as it instantly brightens and lifts the skin under foundation."

Tip 6: Avoid a foundation with SPF in

"I always recommend foundations without SPF for brides to prevent the risk of flashback in flash photography," says Martin. "This is when the SPF in the foundation, usually titanium dioxide, reflects the light and creates a whitish hue to the make-up which can make it look significantly lighter than it does to the naked eye. I suggest brides get their sun protection from a facial SPF under their foundation, instead."
