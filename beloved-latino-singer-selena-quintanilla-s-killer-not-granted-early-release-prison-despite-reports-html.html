Selena Quintanilla's killer Yolanda Saldivar 'NOT granted early prison release'

August 2024 · 3 minute read

The woman who killed Latina singer Selena Quintanilla-Perez will not be granted an early release from prison, despite new reports.

Yolanda Saldivar will remain behind bars at the Gatesville prison in Texas, where she is serving a life sentence for the 1995 murder of the singing sensation.

The false claims stemmed from conservative fake news website The National Report, which said on Monday that Saldivar would be released on January 1 due to medical issues.

According to TMZ, while Saldivar does have some health problems, they are not grave enough to allow for an early release.

What's more, the website reports the 54-year-old has not even filed a petition to be released early and isn't eligible to apply for parole until March 30, 2025.

Saldivar, who was the former president of Selena's fan club, shot and killed the 23-year-old singer on March 31, 1995 in a Corpus Christi hotel room.

Tragic: Saldivar, who was the former president of Selena's fan club, murdered the beloved singer (pictured) on March 31, 1995, where she killed the 23-year-old with one fatal gunshot in a Corpus Christi hotel room

Selena was an award-winning singer, and was named 'top Latin artist of the 90' by Billboard.

The Texas native is also the only female singer to have five albums on the US Billboard 200 chart at the same time.

Her death led to a public outpouring of grief, particularly in the Hispanic community. George W. Bush, then the governor of Texas, declared April 16 - the star's birthday - as Selena Day in the state. 

Breakout role: Jennifer Lopez famously portrayed the late star in the 1997 film Selena, which garnered her a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actress.

Jennifer Lopez famously portrayed the late star in the 1997 film Selena, which garnered her a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actress.

'Getting to play the part of Selena was life-changing for me,' Jennifer told Para Todos magazine earlier this year. 'I got to immerse myself in her life, got to know her family, her home, her culture… every part of her story.'

She continued: 'It was a special time in my life both professionally and personally. Playing her not only opened doors for me in the film world, but it inspired me to start my own music career. In a lot of ways, I wouldn't be where I am today if I hadn't had that experience.'
