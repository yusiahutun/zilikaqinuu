She's famed for performing her burlesque routines in an giant martini glass, but Dita Von Teese cut a much more low-key figure when she did her weekly shop on Saturday. 

The 42-year-old model went out grocery shopping at Gelson's in Los Angeles, where she covered up her figure in a pea-shaped coat. 

The eccentric patchwork number had a wildlife theme running throughout including trees and deer which had been appliqued on the front.

The raven-haired beauty chose not to wear her usual pin-curls, instead tying her locks into a simple slicked back ponytail.

Just before Christmas, the fashion designer shared an adorable snap of herself and older sister Jena in front of a Christmas tree as children.

Dita looks almost unrecognisable as she had strawberry blonde locks instead of the dark tresses she is known for.

She posted the picture with the message: 'I found this holiday pic of me and my sister @jenavonteese. We always had matching dresses on holidays. I think my mother made these.'

Natural beauty: The 42-year-old wore sunglasses and a slick of berry lipstick to complete her look 

Meanwhile, it's not the first time in recent weeks Dita has worn wildlife themed clothing. 

At the end of last month, she was spotted leaving the Vivienne Westwood retail store in Los Angeles on Tuesday in a quirky skirt featuring squirrels on it.

The fun powder blue midi skirt featured embroidered leaves and branches as two of the small woodland animals could be seen holding sequined nuts.
