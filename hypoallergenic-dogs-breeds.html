9 Best Hypoallergenic Dogs - Dog Breeds for People With Allergies

September 2024 · 5 minute read

Do you suffer from animal allergies and want to research the best hypoallergenic dogs to find a breed that won't trigger your reactions? You've come to the right place.

What dogs are hypoallergenic?

"Unfortunately, it's a myth that certain dog breeds or crossbreeds of dogs are hypoallergenic and therefore don't cause allergic reactions in people," Nina Downing, PDSA vet nurse, tells Country Living.

"Some people with allergies may seem to only have symptoms when exposed to certain breeds, however, this doesn't mean that those breeds won't cause a reaction in someone else. Allergens (the substances that cause allergic reactions) can vary between individual pets, even within the same breed so even if you appear not to react to a certain breed, you could still be allergic to an individual dog."

What should you do if you are allergic to dogs?

"Contrary to what a lot of people think, it isn't always dog fur that's the problem. People are actually more commonly allergic to the dander (flakes of dead skin) and proteins from their pets' saliva and skin glands, so hairless breeds can also cause allergies," adds Nina.

"For any prospective dog owners with allergies, the best thing you can do to 'test' out how you'll be with a specific dog, is by spending time with the dog to see if you react. You can do this at a breeder's home, spending time with both the puppies and their mum, or when adopting, rescue centres will often want potential new owners to spend time with the dog they are looking to rehome anyway."

With that in mind, here are nine dog breeds that are better for people who suffer from animal allergies...

1. 'Doodle' Crossbreeds

Wirestock//Getty Images

"Fondly known as Doodles, these dogs are crossed with Poodles. Personalities and fur types will widely depend on what breed the Poodle has been crossed with, but common mixes include the Cockerpoo and Labradoodle," says Nina.

"Depending on the mix, they may shed less than other breeds, due to their Poodle half (but this is not guaranteed, especially with a Labrador in the mix!). Depending on how curly their fur is, they will need daily grooming and will often require frequent clipping."

2. Bedlington Terrier

Sue Thatcher//Getty Images

Graceful and lamb-like, Bedlington Terriers are calm and sweet-natured with owners, and spirited and playful when given the chance. They are reserved with strangers and need plenty of socialisation to help them feel at ease.

3. Bichon Frise

Wirestock//Getty Images

Happy, lively, vivacious and affectionate, Bichon Frise are good-natured dogs that make excellent pets. They are friendly with everyone and they make easy, delightful companions. They are recommended for first-time owners who like to groom their dogs on a daily basis.

4. Chinese Crested (Hairless)

Robbie Goodall&#xA;www.robbiegoodall.com//Getty Images

"Known for being hairless and sweet, you're less likely to find fur around the house if you've got a Chinese Crested, as they are lacking in the fur department to begin with," continues Nina.

"Whilst they may not need trips to the groomer specifically, owners often do pay them a visit so they can be bathed, stray hairs trimmed and have skin treatments applied, as they are prone to skin problems. These dogs can be sensitive to changes in the elements so they may need help to maintain a comfortable body temperature. You'll need to be sure to protect their skin in the sunshine too."

5. Maltese

VladimirCizmar / 500px//Getty Images

Lively, alert, gentle, and sweet-natured, Maltese are sociable and enjoy company. They are willing to please but can be a little slow to learn what is required. They are happy and friendly to all and affectionate.

6. Schnauzer

Aleksandar Gazibara//Getty Images

Alert, reliable, calm and inquisitive, the Schnauzer enjoys company but can bark excessively and can be territorial.

Nina tells us: "Miniature or not, Schnauzers are gentle, yet playful and inquisitive dogs. Grooming is needed two or three times each week, and they'll need to visit the groomers to be hand stripped, although some owners choose to have them clipped."

7. Poodle

Alessandra Sawick//Getty Images

"Poodles are intelligent, playful and adventurous, who enjoy company. They need daily grooming to keep their coat in tip-top condition and will often also require regular clipping and shaping by a groomer throughout the year as well," says Nina.

8. Portuguese Water Dog

Ines Arnshoff • www.ines-arnshoff.de//Getty Images

As the name suggests, Portuguese Water Dogs have a water-resistant coat that sheds minimally, making them a top choice for allergy sufferers. Originally bred to assist fishermen, these dogs are active, and intelligent, and thrive in households with active lifestyles.

9. Havanese

Dorottya_Mathe//Getty Images

"Havanese are lively, affectionate and intelligent dogs. Although small they should not be considered delicate as they enjoy boisterous play! They do have a long, soft coat which requires daily grooming to prevent knots and tangles," says Nina.

"Their fur can quite easily become matted if not maintained and may require trips to a groomer throughout the year as well."
