Kristin Cavallari's marriage with Jay Cutler ended over the ex-NFL star being 'rude towards family'

February 2024 · 6 minute read

The real reason behind Kristin Cavallari's divorce from Jay Cutler was his 'rude' behavior not only towards fans, but with her, their family and friends, ultimately leaving his wife feeling alone with their three children, DailyMail.com can exclusively reveal.

The reality star couple announced their shock split in late April, shortly after returning from a family vacation in the Bahamas. Cutler, 37, filed first, wanting primary custody. Cavallari, 33, hit back days later, claiming he was refusing to leave their $4.2 million Nashville home.

But despite reports that Cavallari was frustrated with Cutler for being unmotivated after his 12-year NFL career, friends close to the couple said that wasn't the case, instead Cavallari was over his bad attitude.

One told DailyMail.com: 'Kristin never had an issue with Jay for being lazy, this wasn't the problem at all. Those stories are all BS.

'Kristin's issue was how he behaved with her, their family, friends and fans. That was the problem that caused their marriage to shut down.

'He would be really rude and dismissive of anyone who would approach her.'

'Kristin is smart and knows how important her fans are. She values their support and is beyond grateful for them. Jay simply had no time or tolerance for them and this would embarrass her.'

Throughout Cutler's NFL career, playing as a quarterback for the Chicago Bears, Denver Broncos and Miami Dolphins, he was dogged by reports labeling him a 'jerk'.

Cutler's former Broncos teammate Champ Bailey agreed in an interview that he was a 'jerk', saying: 'Absolutely. And it's not just the guys in the locker room. He rubbed a lot of people the wrong way.

'Certain teammates, you know, like maybe a backup quarterback… didn't like him. Other guys that were on the roster at the time. Guys that really have nothing good to say about him.'

An insider said Cavallari 'found that the perception of him as an NFL player, wasn't a perception at all.'

They added: 'It was how he acted throughout their entire marriage and that's what caused it to end. Once a jerk, always a jerk.'

The couple first began dating in 2010 after meeting at a Chicago Bears preseason game, getting married in 2013. They share sons Camden Jack, seven, Jaxon Wyatt, six, and daughter Saylor James, four.

Cavallari often posted loved up photos of the two on her Instagram and they had legions of fans gushing over their relationship, which was highlighted on their reality show Very Cavallari.

But a source explained: 'As the years went on, Cutler stopped having any time for Kristin. He pushed her away, made her feel isolated. Even when they were in the same house, she felt alone with their children.

'He'd always be in a bad mood and closed off. There was ice running through his veins and he'd couldn't have been colder or more distant. 

'His behavior when they filmed her reality show was at an all-time low.

'If he wasn't happy with how something had come across, he would take his microphone off and storm off.

'This horrified Kristin and he was lucky that she was an executive producer as it meant that those scenes and his horrible behavior never made it on television.'

The former couple is now battling it out in court over child custody and finances.

In Cutler's original filing, he argued for primary custody, saying he was the 'available at home parent and the primary caretaker of the parties' minor children', despite reportedly having previously agreed to sharing equal care of their brood.
