Who is Jenelle Potter?

The Potter family case brought significant attention to the sleepy and friendly town of Mountain City in Tennessee. Which makes one wonder about the gruesomeness of the crime to make it a headliner. The back story is quite fascinating, and it involves social media and jealousy that turned deadly.

Jenelle Potter was born in 1981 and lived with her parents and her younger sibling in the small town where they moved in 2004. Her parents were highly protective of her and watched her every move. Jenelle's father was a former marine who worked briefly for the CIA after serving in Vietnam and retired to a quiet life.

On the other hand, Jenelle's mother, Barbara, worked at Hewlett-Packard. Unfortunately, Jenelle had several health concerns and was living a sheltered life. She experienced learning difficulties and had hearing difficulties. Also, she was diagnosed with Type 1 diabetes when she was small.

What sentence did the perpetrators get?

The entire Potter family, except their youngest daughter, were convicted for the murder of Billy Payne and Billie Jean Hayworth. In October 2013, Marvin was sentenced to two life sentences. On the other hand, Jamie entered a plea deal and got 25 years.

Jenelle and Barbara went to trial in May 2015. And after court deliberations, they were found guilty of first-degree murder and sentenced to life in prison.

Post-conviction hearing on the case

There was a post-conviction hearing for Barbara and Jenelle in 2021. According to Marvin's testimony, he and only him murdered the two victims. And when Marvin was asked if he accidentally cut Billy's throat, he responded that he did it on purpose.

The judge granted Barbara a new trial stating that the lawyer, who had represented her husband at a previous trial, had a conflict of interest by acting as an attorney for both.
