The Birth of 21 Savage

21 Savage, whose real name is Shéyaa Bin Abraham-Joseph, was born on October 22, 1992, in Plaistow, Newham, London, England.

Relocating to Atlanta

At the age of 7, 21 Savage moved to Atlanta, Georgia, with his mother, where he would later immerse himself in the city’s vibrant music scene.

Early Experiences with Music

21 Savage’s passion for music began at a young age, inspired by artists such as Gucci Mane and Future.

The Influence of Street Life

Growing up in a rough neighborhood in Atlanta had a profound impact on 21 Savage, and his music often reflects the harsh realities of street life.

Mixtape Debut

In 2015, 21 Savage made his mixtape debut with “The Slaughter Tape,” which gained him significant attention in the hip-hop community.

Collaboration with Metro Boomin

21 Savage’s collaboration with renowned producer Metro Boomin resulted in the critically acclaimed mixtape “Savage Mode” in 2016.

Breakthrough Success

His breakthrough hit single, “X” featuring Future, received widespread acclaim and further propelled 21 Savage into the limelight.

Personal Struggles

21 Savage has been open about his personal struggles, including dealing with poverty and being involved in street violence.

Philanthropic Ventures

Despite his challenging upbringing, 21 Savage remains committed to giving back to his community by supporting various educational and financial initiatives.

The Story Behind the Dagger Tattoo

The dagger tattoo on 21 Savage’s forehead holds symbolic significance, representing a promise to himself to always stay sharp and focused.

Grammy Award-Winning Song

21 Savage won a Grammy Award in 2020 for his collaboration with J. Cole on the song “A Lot,” which addresses social and political issues.

UK and US Dual Citizenship

Despite being primarily associated with the Atlanta music scene, 21 Savage holds dual citizenship in the United Kingdom and the United States.

Interest in Philosophy

21 Savage has expressed an interest in philosophy and often incorporates introspective and thought-provoking elements into his music.

Sports Enthusiast

Beyond music, 21 Savage is a passionate sports fan, particularly supporting Atlanta-based teams such as the Falcons and Hawks.

Acting Stint

21 Savage made his acting debut with a cameo appearance in the critically acclaimed film “American Honey” in 2016.

Connection with Issa Rae

21 Savage has collaborated with actress and writer Issa Rae on the soundtrack for the hit HBO show “Insecure.
