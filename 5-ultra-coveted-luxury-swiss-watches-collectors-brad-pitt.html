5 ultra-coveted luxury Swiss watches for collectors like Brad Pitt, starring models from Jaeger-LeCo

February 2024 · 2 minute read

Luxury watches are crafted with precision, for excellence and endurance, and backed by many years of tradition. These are some of the reasons timepieces have remained so cherished over the years, so collectable across generations, immune to fickle passing trends.

Here STYLE gathers watches tailored for all occasions from Jaeger-LeCoultre, Panerai, Richard Mille, Rolex and Speake-Marin: some suited to the pressures of diving, others to those of the C-suite, or to the need to make an impression at social functions. All might make a memorable addition to your dream watch collection.

5 Rolex watch launches for spring/summer 2021

Jaeger-LeCoultre

The Master Ultra Thin Tourbillon Moon reveals Jaeger-LeCoultre’s new self-winding Calibre 983 to the world. In addition, the watch combines a moon phase and a tourbillon alongside the brand’s “jumping” date complication. US$88,500.

Panerai

The Luminor Marina PAM01117 is elegant yet functional, illuminated in the dark – a speciality of the brand – and manufactured with a 3D printing technique known as metal laser sintering. Price upon request.

Richard Mille

Introducing the RM 07-01 Automatic Starry Night, with its scattering of 181 diamond “stars” against a matt Carbon TPT “night sky” bezel and dial, the perfect ornament for an evening out. Price upon request.

Rolex

With its precise dual time zone display, the Rolex Sky-Dweller is ideal for an international business person working across time zones. The 18-carat patented Everose gold case and scratch-resistant sapphire crystal makes sure it stays the course and always looks good, US$40,000.

Speake-Marin

Limited to 15 pieces, the London Chronograph Triple revisits the beloved Valjoux 88 Calibre chronograph model with its recognisable column-wheel, three counters and iconic titanium Piccadilly case. Price upon request.
