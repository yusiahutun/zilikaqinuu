Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are revamping the way technology is being utilized to aid an increasing range of real-world problems. It is important that these solutions consistently deliver accurate, logical, and useful outcomes. If a business still ignores such futuristic technology, then it might be holding its growth back. Data Annotation is seen as a critically important foundation for such solutions.

In this domain, Mr. Neville Patel, a visionary leader, and his team of experts at Qualitas Global Services are making strong in-roads to help businesses transform themselves and ensure they are future-ready by providing world-class services in the Data Annotations and Tagging business.

Qualitas Global Services was founded in 2014 by Mr. Neville Patel, a Six Sigma Black Belt. With his creative approach and strong work ethic, he believes in quality work paired with a customer-centric approach.

In our talk with Mr. Neville Patel, CEO at Qualitas Global Services, we picked his brains on the current industry scenario and what Qualitas Global offers to it’s customers.  

What is Data Annotation?

Data Annotation is an extremely detail-focused, unavoidably manual, rigorous, and time-consuming process that helps in comprehensively identifying, categorizing, labeling, and organizing the data captured in various formats including images, videos, voice, and text.

It is used to “train” the AI or ML algorithms on an ongoing basis. Data annotation ushers in the required accuracy of the algorithm and improves end-user outcomes.

Many companies don’t want to invest in creating in-house data annotation teams. They want to focus on their core product/app development and hence they look to partner with companies like Qualitas Global Services who can quickly scale to thousands of people, provide data security to the information provided, have the domain expertise, and are cost-efficient.

When people say it is impossible, remember it is impossible for them, not you.

What were the challenges you faced initially since both Qualitas Global Services and this business was very new in the past decade?

I would say my main challenge was to find the right people and the right management team who could understand and buy into my vision. Luckily this problem was quickly resolved and with the right people (and ethics) we quickly grew from a 6 people company to where we are today.

Our customers trusted us enough to train our core people into different varieties of complex annotations and then we set up a strong training team to further train the rest of the company.

Which was that point would you say that Qualitas Global Services reached a growth that gave you a sense of satisfaction?

We understood that the big changes do not happen overnight, but big changes can and should be broken into small attainable parts. The entire management team planned on the future and set on a quest to achieve this.

We grew substantially when we onboarded a Fortune 3 company which also helped us strengthen our Data Security policies, our hiring practices and made sure we were always on our toes.

This inspired confidence in all our other customers plus helped my team get into the big league.

To know that my core team still comprises of day one employees who have learned and grown with me in the process gives me immense satisfaction.

Whose business story do you find the most inspiring?
