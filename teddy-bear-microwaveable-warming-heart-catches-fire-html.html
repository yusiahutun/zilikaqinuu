Teddy bear with 'microwaveable warming heart' catches fire

July 2024 Â· 3 minute read

A microwaveable teddy bear that supposedly makes a 'great bedtime snuggle buddy' caught fire in a baby's crib.

Newly married Jessica, 28, and Royal Deal, 31, had given the bear to their six-month-old son, Raiden, who has a respiratory virus that affects his breathing.

The 'Evri Cuddle Buddy Bear' bear was a gift from Mr Deal's mother and boasts of providing 'natural relief of aches and pains and aids in relaxation'.

Mr Deal, a car salesman, had put the teddy in the microwave for 30 seconds and then placed it in Raiden's crib to try to help him sleep.

Filled with a blend of cinnamon, clove and eucalyptus, it is supposed to help relax children in bed.

Mr and Mrs Deal had put the 'snuggle buddy' bear in Raiden's crib every night for the previous two weeks.

However on Saturday morning at 2am the couple were woken by the smell of fire in their home in Show Low, Arizona.

Rushing into their son's room, they realized the source of the fire was the teddy.

Mrs Deal told MailOnline: 'My husband put the centre of the bear [that comes out] in the microwave for 30 seconds. It wasn't really hot, it wasn't on fire, he didn't have any burns, so he put it back in the bear and then in Raiden's crib.

'Then about 2am in the morning I woke up to the smell of smoke. I first went into the check my other children, who are aged eight and five, but there was nothing there.

'My husband and I then went into the room where the crib is and it was really smoky...but Raiden was still sleeping. 

'My husband grabbed Raiden, I then saw the teddy had burnt through his crib shoot, through the sheets and onto the mattress. 

'I picked up the teddy and put it in the sink and it had full embers and was smouldering.'  

Raiden was amazingly unharmed and he has since been moved into the living room. 

The couple, who married on May 21 last year, now want the toy removed from shops and to make other families aware of the risk.

She took the teddy bear back to the Walmart in Show Low where she purchased it.

The mother-of-three filed a claim, which will now be sent to the manufacturer. 

She told MailOnline: 'This bear is marketed towards children that need to be helped. If my son had been holding the bear it could have caused a lot of harm. I'm just so grateful it didn't get that bad. 

'You can't really stay up all night watching and making sure that these toys that you trust are OK. It's all up to the manufacturer now. 

'I have still not been able to speak with the manufacturer and it is still on the shelf at the store when I bought it.'

MailOnline have contacted Evri for a response.
