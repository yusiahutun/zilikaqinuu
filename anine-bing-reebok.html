The surprising muse behind Anine Bings collaboration with Reebok

June 2024 · 2 minute read

Fashion collaborations are big news this year: and the partnerships are becoming more and more creative. Earlier this month, Mango announced its forthcoming capsule collection designed by Victoria Beckham – a move to bring the designer’s feminine tailoring to the masses, while H&M has turned its attention to the niche arena, linking up with cult favourites Rokh.

Now, Anine Bing x Reebok has landed, offering a tight edit of off-duty staples that combine the sportswear giant’s athletic cuts with the designer’s cool Californian DNA. Looking to classic Reebok activewear pieces from the Eighties and Nineties, the collection features oversized sweatshirts contrasted against sculpting bodysuits and bike shorts – as well as a few stellar accessories.

courtesy anine bing

While the brand cites ‘vintage muses’ as the primary source of inspiration, there’s one woman in particular that surely sparked the sweat-and-shorts combination. No one mastered the pairing in a more iconic way than Princess Diana, who was often seen off-duty in a collegiate sweatshirt, three sizes too big, balanced by a pair of sculpting sportswear shorts.

JOHNNY EGGITT//Getty Images

The collection also pays homage to Diana's predilections for pairing tailoring with off-duty separates: Bing's signature oversized blazer appears to sharpen up the slouchy joggers and soft separates.

There are shoes too: three classic Reebok styles have been re-worked, including the Freestyle Hi, the Classic Nylon trainer and the Classic slide, meaning your new weekend uniform is complete from head to toe. (Well, you may want to add a pair of Bing’s slick black sunglasses.)
