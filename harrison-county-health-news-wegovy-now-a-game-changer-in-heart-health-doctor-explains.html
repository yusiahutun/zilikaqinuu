Key Findings for you in for Harrison County:

Background

Semaglutide has been demonstrated to lower the risk of cardiovascular events, such as heart attacks and strokes, in patients who have been diagnosed with diabetes.

The purpose of the study was to determine if semaglutide, a medication known to reduce cardiovascular risk in patients with diabetes, could also lower the risk of cardiovascular events in individuals who are overweight or obese but do not have diabetes.

Methods

Multicenter, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled, event-driven superiority trial.

Enrolled patients: 45 years or older with pre-existing cardiovascular disease. Their BMI was greater than or equal to 27 and they had no history of diabetes.

Results

Total patients enrolled: 17,604 (8,803 were on semaglutide and 8,801 received a placebo..

Mean exposure duration: 34.2 months.

Mean follow-up duration: 39.8 months.

The primary goal was to observe cardiovascular events (such as heart attacks, strokes, or cardiovascular-related deaths). In the semaglutide group, 6.5% of participants experienced such an event. In the placebo group, 8.0% of the people experienced a cardiovascular event.

Conclusions

In patients with pre-existing cardiovascular disease, overweight or obesity, but without diabetes, weekly subcutaneous semaglutide (2.4 mg) was superior to placebo in reducing the incidence of cardiac issues. In other words, Semaglutide is shown to be effective in managing heart disease for people without diabetes!
