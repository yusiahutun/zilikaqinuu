Fun Fact

Though most of us tend to associate perfumes with feminine scents and colognes with masculine scents, those labels actually have nothing to do with scent. There are five main categories of fragrances: pure perfumes, eau de parfums, eau de toilettes, eau de colognes, and eau fraîches. What each label refers to is the concentration of natural and synthetic perfume oils, or — in essence — how strong the scent will be. Cologne actually refers to the category with a light concentration of perfume oils.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What Is the Difference Between Perfume and Cologne?

    Cologne actually refers to the category with a light concentration of perfume oils, typically 2% to 4%, whereas pure perfume is between 15% to 30%. Colognes are cut with more alcohol, so they only last a few hours and are meant to be sprayed a few times throughout the day to freshen up.

  • Is Cologne Just for Men?

    Cologne isn’t specifically for men or women (in fact, perfume isn’t, either!). The labels on fragrances, including words like pure perfume, eau de parfums, eau de toilettes, eau de colognes, and eau fraîches all refer to the concentration of fragrance in the product — not the type of scent or who it’s for.

  • What Brands Will You Get in a Cologne Subscription Box?

    Many cologne subscription boxes feature designer brands, like Jimmy Choo, Versace, Valentino, Gucci, Dolce & Gabbana, and more. But others, like Skylar, offer original (and clean) scents, while companies like Olfactif offer unique, niche brands instead of designer scents.

  • What Size Samples Do You Get in a Cologne Subscription Box?

    Most cologne subscription boxes include samples sized at eight milliliters or 0.27 oz. Some are slightly larger, like LUXSB’s nine-milliliter (0.30 oz) bottles. Others are smaller but have more spritzers in each box, like Debonair Scent’s three bottles of three-milliliter fragrances per month.

  • Do Cologne Subscription Boxes Include Fake Scents?

    All of the cologne subscription boxes on our list promise 100% authentic scents. With anything being repackaged, there’s a level of risk, so it’s important to read reviews. Every box on our list has thousands of positive reviews to avoid this issue.

  • How Can You Make Your Fragrance Last Throughout the Day?

    One of the best ways to make your fragrance last is to moisturize the skin first; apply a dab of lotion (or better yet, Aquaphor or Vaseline) to give the fragrance something to grip onto. Don’t be afraid to reapply throughout the day: Cologne has a lower fragrance concentration, so it’s meant to be reapplied.
