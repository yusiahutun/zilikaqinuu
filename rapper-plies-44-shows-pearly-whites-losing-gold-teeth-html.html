Rapper Plies, 44, shows off his pearly whites after losing gold teeth

June 2024 · 4 minute read

He is known for his shining gold teeth, but now he has something new to smile about.

The rapper Plies lit up social media on Friday morning when he debuted a set of brilliant white teeth in a beaming selfie.

The 44-year-old rhyme-maker commemorated his new smile by burying his removed gold crowns in a hilarious symbolic funeral.

New smile: The rapper Plies, 44, debuted a brilliant white set of teeth on Twitter on Friday morning after getting his gold crowns removed

Plies (real name: Algernod Lanier Washington) snapped his selfie from inside his car while wearing a simple black T-shirt and an olive ski cap.

'I Just Showed My Mom My New Smile. She Hugged Me Started Crying & Said "I Finally Got My Baby Back,"' he gushed in his caption.

Earlier on Thursday, Plies bid his gold teeth farewell by burying them in a shallow grave.

'I Just Removed & Buried My Gold Teeth!!!!!!' he enthused in a short video posted to Twitter.

'I Never Thought In A Million Years This Day Would Come!! I Wanted Elevation So I Stepped Out On Faith!!!'

He also took the opportunity to add some of his signature raunchiness.

'I Ate Some Good P***y With Them Teeth Doe!!!! I Heard Women Love Men With Pretty Smiles!!!!' he wrote, before urging his followers to '#StayTuned.' 

Dramatic farewell: He scooped dirt over the crowns with a tiny spoon while playing somber music from his phone

In the clip, which was scored with somber music that sounded as if it was playing from his phone, he said, 'It's a sad day today, y'all. It's a very sad day.

'We're all gathered here today to bury my gold teeth,' he said dramatically while jingling the crowns around in a tooth-shaped plastic container.

'Been a long time coming,' he continued as he held up one of the crowns.

'I done ate some good p***y with these teeth right here. Some good p***y,' he said as someone off camera laughed. 'But like they say, all great things must come to an end.'

The hilarious clip climaxed with the musician shoveling a thin layer of dirt over the crowns with a tiny spoon.
