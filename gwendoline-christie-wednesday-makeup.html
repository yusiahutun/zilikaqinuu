Gwendoline Christie make-up in Netfix's Wednesday

August 2024 · 2 minute read

Television characters are increasingly inspiring fashion and beauty trends, and Gwendoline Christie's transformation for Netflix hit Wednesday is the latest on-screen look grabbing the attention of lipstick fans. Starring as Larissa Weems, the principal of Nevermore Academy, Christie has since revealed that the role was her first experience feeling "genuinely beautiful" on screen.

The actress thanked director Tim Burton and costume designer Colleen Atwood, as well as the project's hair and make-up team for her "transformation" in a recent interview.

"Colleen Atwood is rightfully a legend, and what she does is close to witchcraft in terms of transformation," she told Entertainment Weekly. "It is an honour of my life to work with Colleen and to work with Tim."

Netflix

Of Atwood she added: "The way she made me feel was my body felt celebrated and beautiful. Never once did I feel like there was something to hide or something to be ashamed of. She made me feel incredible. She was also enormously collaborative and wanted to know what I thought."

When it came to her beauty look, make-up artist Tara McDonald drew inspiration from Hitchcock heroines, creating a 1950s-style aesthetic with a strong brow, precise black eyeliner and of course, a strong crimson lip. The MUA name-checked two particular shades that she used on Christie: Lisa Eldridge's True Velvet Lipstick in Velvet Ribbon and Code8's Matte Velour Lipstick in Opera.

Matte Velour in "Opera" Code8
Rouge À Lèvres Mat Matte Lipstick Gucci
Hot Lips 2.0 in "Patsy Red" Charlotte Tilbury

Daring statement lips were a consistent theme throughout the filming of the series; Jenna Ortega's Wednesday sported a signature lip courtesy of MAC's Lip Pencil in "Nightmoth", diffused with clear lip balm. Meanwhile, Catherine Zeta Jones, who stars as Morticia Addams, showcased a bruised plum credited as Tom Ford by McDonald, via Instagram.

Christie's costume was made complete by Weems' impeccable Italian wig, which was reportedly wet set every single night to maintain its pristine finish.

Tim Burton served as executive producer on the series, directing four out of eight episodes, while Christina Ricci, whose breakout role as Wednesday in the 1991 The Addams Family film, also made a special appearance.
